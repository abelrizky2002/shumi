# Personal Audio Journey: The Budget King Search

### Current Daily Driver: Tangzu Wan'er S.G.
After testing several entry-level IEMs and standard headsets, I settled on the **Tangzu Wan'er S.G.**. Here’s my personal take on why it stands out:

- **Sound Signature**: Unlike my previous standard headsets that felt muddy, the Wan'er provides a much cleaner vocal clarity. The "Warm-Neutral" tuning makes it very comfortable for long coding sessions or listening to J-Pop/Anime soundtracks without ear fatigue.
- **Comparison**: Compared to my old gaming headsets, the instrument separation here is a game-changer. I can actually hear the distinct layers in a track rather than just a wall of bass.
- **Build & Comfort**: The shell design fits my ears perfectly. Even though it's budget-friendly, it doesn't feel "cheap" when worn.

### The DAC Dilemma
I've been researching how much a dedicated **DAC (Digital-to-Analog Converter)** actually impacts sound quality for entry-level IEMs.
- **Current Observation**: Direct output from my laptop is "okay," but I notice some floor noise (hissing) during quiet parts of a song.
- **Future Goal**: I’m planning to test a simple Dongle DAC (like the CX31993 or Dawn Pro) to see if it actually cleans up the signal or if it's just a placebo effect for budget gear.

### Summary
For me, audio isn't just about hearing music; it's about the technical satisfaction of finding the best price-to-performance ratio. The Tangzu Wan'er is currently the benchmark for my ears.
